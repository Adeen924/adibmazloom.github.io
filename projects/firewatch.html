The Concept
ONE SYSTEM,
MANY VEHICLES

Most drone programs design one vehicle for one mission. FireWatch is built around a different idea: a mothership that extends the operational range, endurance, and capability of smaller payload drones by serving as a mobile base.

The mothership handles the heavy lifting — power, transport, coordination. The payload drones handle the mission — lightweight, fast, deployable on demand. Together they do what neither vehicle could accomplish alone.

The system architecture, deployment mechanism, and vehicle integration approach are what the provisional patent covers — the core insight that makes this work.

01
Extended Range
Mothership transports payload drones to operational areas, dramatically extending effective range beyond battery limitations of the payload vehicles alone.
02
Fleet Scalability
One mothership, multiple payload drones. The system scales by adding vehicles rather than replacing the entire platform.
03
Mission Flexibility
Payload drones can be swapped for different mission profiles. The mothership platform stays constant while the payload adapts.
04
Autonomous Coordination
Deployment, recovery, and power management designed for autonomous operation — minimal human intervention during the mission.
Vehicle 01
MOTHERSHIP —
DESIGN & BUILD
  • 🏗️
    Airframe Design — Structural frame engineered for heavy-lift with payload integration points. Trade studies across materials and geometry to optimize strength-to-weight.
  • ⚙️
    Propulsion System — Motor selection, mounting structures, and ESC integration sized for combined mothership + payload drone weight budget.
  • ⚡
    Power Delivery — Power architecture supports both mothership flight systems and payload drone charging during transport.
  • 🔗
    Payload Integration — Mechanical and electrical interface for payload drone docking, retention, and release — the core novel architecture.
  • 🧪
    V1 Status — Functional prototype built and flight-tested. Structural performance and payload carrying validated. Iteration underway on integration interface.
Vehicle 02
PAYLOAD DRONE —
IN DEVELOPMENT

The payload drone is the second vehicle in the system — currently in early design stage in SolidWorks. Where the mothership prioritizes lift capacity and endurance, the payload drone is optimized for agility, low weight, and mission adaptability.

The design constraints are driven by the integration interface with the mothership — the payload drone has to fit, dock, undock, and operate within the power and weight budget the mothership can support.

This is an active design effort. The architecture is defined; the detailed mechanical design is in progress.
